# ISO-NE — Generator Interconnection Requirements

## 1. Process overview & timeline

- Historically serial (queue-position) under **Schedule 22 (Large, > 20 MW)** and **Schedule 23 (Small, ≤ 20 MW)**; Order 2023 compliance introduces **cluster study windows** with readiness requirements.
- Flow: Interconnection Request → scoping meeting → **System Impact Study** (cluster) → decision point → **Facilities Study** → **Interconnection Agreement** (LGIA/SGIA).
- Two service levels: **Capacity Network Resource Capability (CNRC)** — required to participate as capacity in the Forward Capacity Market — and **Network Resource Capability (NRC)** for energy-only. CNRC requests trigger additional deliverability review.
- Typical IR-to-IA duration 2–3 years for large facilities.

## 2. Application / entry requirements

- Interconnection Request with:
  - Completed IR form (Schedule 22 Appendix 1 / Schedule 23 equivalents)
  - Study deposit (see §3)
  - **Site control** evidence or deposit in lieu where permitted (Order 2023 tightened this to 100% footprint at application)
  - Requested capability (CNRC vs NRC), POI, in-service date
- Scoping meeting scheduled after validation; deficiency cure windows apply.

## 3. Deposits, fees & milestones

- **Study deposits**: initial deposit at IR (indicative $50k–$250k+ scaled by size/track; actual study costs invoiced), further deposits at System Impact and Facilities Study agreements.
- Order 2023 compliance adds **commercial readiness deposits** escalating through decision points, forfeited on an escalating schedule on withdrawal.
- Financial security for network upgrades at IA execution.

## 4. Site control requirements

- **100% site control for the generating facility footprint at application** (post-Order-2023), term through COD; lease/option/deed with legal description.
- Gen-tie and interconnection facilities control by Facilities Study.
- Regulatory-barrier exceptions (e.g., state-controlled offshore wind lease areas) use deposit-in-lieu provisions.

## 5. Technical data & modeling requirements

- **PSS/E** power flow and dynamics conforming to ISO-NE base cases and **Planning Procedure PP5-6** model requirements; MMWG-format compatibility.
- IBRs: approved library models (REGC/REEC/REPC) or accepted vendor models with source data; **PSCAD** requested for weak-grid or high-IBR areas.
- Reactive capability ±0.95 PF at POI (Order 827), PFR (Order 842), ride-through per PRC-024 / IEEE 2800 adoption.
- Short circuit data; protection coordination data for the interconnecting TO.
- One-line diagram (breaker-level), GSU parameters, metering plan (revenue-quality metering per OP-18).
- Storage/hybrid: MWh, charge/discharge ratings, charging source; FCM participation drives extra data (CNRC).

## 6. Required documents checklist

1. Interconnection Request form (Schedule 22/23)
2. Study deposit confirmation
3. Site control evidence (100% footprint)
4. Signatory/entity documentation
5. PSS/E raw + dyr models (PP5-6 compliant)
6. PSCAD package (if requested)
7. Reactive capability curves
8. One-line diagram
9. Site plan / boundary map
10. Equipment data sheets (inverter, GSU, BESS)
11. CNRC/NRC election

## 7. Withdrawal & penalties

- Withdrawal refunds unexpended study deposits; readiness deposits forfeit on an escalating schedule at decision points (post-Order-2023).
- Non-responsive or deficient requests removed after cure periods; restudy cost exposure for late-stage withdrawals.

## 8. Common deficiency triggers

- Models not conforming to PP5-6 (top validation failure).
- Site control term/acreage gaps.
- CNRC requested without deliverability-supporting data.
- One-line missing metering or demarcation detail.
- MW inconsistencies between IR, one-line, and equipment schedule.
