# MISO — Generator Interconnection Requirements

## 1. Process overview & timeline

Total from application deadline to GIA ≈ **373 days** (DPP Phase 1 + 2 + 3 + GIA):

| Stage | Duration | Key events |
|---|---|---|
| Application review | 90 days | Scoping call (day ~40), screening analysis results (~15), site control review #1 report (~30). IC may withdraw prior to DPP Phase 1 kickoff with 100% of M2 returned. |
| **DPP Phase 1** | ~100 days | A10 model review (10 BD), preliminary DPP SIS (65 days), **IC Decision Point #1** (15 BD) — continue and pay M3, or withdraw (AWP: MISO keeps 10% of M2, 50% of M2 at risk). |
| **DPP Phase 2** | ~105 days | Model review (6 BD), revised DPP SIS (75 days), **IC Decision Point #2** (15 BD) — continue and pay M4, or withdraw (AWP: 35% of M2, 65% of M2 at risk, 100% of M3 returned). MISO must receive Affected System (AFS) reports prior to SIS posting. |
| **DPP Phase 3** | ~60 days | Model review (6 BD), final DPP SIS (50 days). Milestone true-down. AWP on withdrawal: 75% of M2; 25% of M2 and 100% of M3+M4 become at risk upon entry to Phase 3. |
| **GIA** | ~135 days | Facilities Study review, tender draft GIA, IC executes, Transmission Owner executes; MISO files GIA. Site Control Evidence Submission #3 prior to GIA signing (100% of ICIF and TOIF/NU where applicable). |

- Notes: Affected System, Stability, and Short Circuit studies are required only for DPP Phase 2 and Phase 3 (since Sept 19, 2018 cycles). Days are calendar days unless marked BD (business days).

## 2. Application / entry requirements

- Complete DPP application by the **cycle application deadline** with milestone **M1 satisfied** (technical requirements, D1 + D2 fees) and **M2 paid**.
- One POI per request; provisional requests allowed with adjusted milestones.
- Site control evidence submission #1 due at application (see §4); site control documentation for Review #1 due at least 90 days prior to DPP Phase 1 kickoff (from DPP-2020 cycle onward).

## 3. Deposits, fees & milestones

| Milestone | Amount | When |
|---|---|---|
| **M1** | Technical requirements, D1 (application fee) + D2 (DPP study funding deposit) | At application |
| **M2** | **$8,000/MW** | At application (100% returned if withdrawn before DPP Phase 1 kickoff) |
| **M3** | Greater of (20% of NU − M2) or **$1,000/MW** | Decision Point #1 (pay to continue into Phase 2) |
| **M4** | (30% of NU) − M3 − M2 | Decision Point #2 (pay to continue into Phase 3) |

- NU = network upgrade cost estimate from the current study phase; M3/M4 are adjusted (trued-down/up) as NU estimates are revised.
- True-down of milestone payments occurs in Phase 3.

## 4. Site control requirements

- At application: **100% site control for the generating facility** and **50% site control for the generating facility's interconnection facilities tie line**, or an **$80,000/mile deposit** in lieu for the tie line (evidence submission #1).
- Site control evidence submission #2 during DPP (tie line site control 100%).
- Site control evidence submission #3 prior to GIA signing: **100% of IC interconnection facilities**, and TO interconnection facilities / network upgrade rights-of-way where applicable.
- Acceptable evidence: executed lease/option/deed with legal description matching the project footprint and term through COD.

## 5. Technical data & modeling requirements

- Power flow and dynamics in **PSS/E** format consistent with MISO MOD (Model on Demand) data requirements; A10 model review at Phase 1 start.
- IBRs: acceptable dynamic models (second-generation renewable models REGC/REEC/REPC or vendor user models with documentation); PSCAD models on request for stability-constrained areas.
- Reactive capability ±0.95 PF at POI (Order 827), PFR settings (Order 842).
- Short circuit data, stability data for Phases 2–3.
- SLD with POI, metering, demarcation, relaying; equipment ratings (GSU MVA/%Z, inverter counts and model).

## 6. Required documents checklist

1. DPP application (queue submission) with D1 application fee
2. D2 study funding deposit and M2 wire confirmation
3. Site control evidence #1 (facility 100%, tie line 50% or $80K/mile deposit)
4. Legal entity / signatory documentation
5. PSS/E power flow case data (MOD submission)
6. Dynamic model files and documentation
7. Reactive capability curves
8. Single-line diagram
9. Site plan and project boundary map (KMZ or parcel map)
10. Equipment specification sheets (inverter, GSU, BESS if applicable)
11. Provisional request adjustments (if applicable)

## 7. Withdrawal & penalties (Automatic Withdrawal Penalty — AWP)

- Before DPP Phase 1 kickoff: **100% of M2 returned**.
- Withdraw at Decision Point #1: MISO AWP **10% of M2**; MISO holds 50% of M2 at risk.
- Withdraw at Decision Point #2: AWP **35% of M2**, 65% of M2 at risk; 100% of M3 returned.
- Withdraw in/after Phase 3: AWP **75% of M2**; 25% of M2 and 100% of M3 and M4 at risk upon entry to Phase 3.
- Milestone and M2-50%-at-risk provisions apply beginning with the DPP-2020 cycle.
- DPP Cycle 2 Phase 3 starts only after Cycle 1 GIAs are executed or filed unexecuted with FERC.

## 8. Common deficiency triggers

- Site control acreage/term not matching application MW or COD.
- Missing tie-line site control (or deposit) at evidence submission #1.
- Model data failing A10 review (bus mismatches, unsupported dynamic models).
- MW inconsistencies between application, one-line, and equipment schedule.
- Missing AFS data responses for Phase 2 posting.
